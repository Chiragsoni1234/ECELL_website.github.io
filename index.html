ABOUT ECELL



"E-Cell MMMUT helps the hustling startups and young professionals via dynamic workshops, thought-provoking speaker sessions, high-stakes business plan competitions, and numerous other game-changing initiatives throughout the year to create a crucible for innovation. We stand as pillars of support for budding entrepreneurs, providing them with personalized guidance from experienced mentors, crucial funding opportunities, and a robust network that can change the course of their journey forever!"

Dr.Ram Bilas Prasad

Faculty Advisor

I am thrilled to extend a warm welcome to all the students, mentors, and enthusiasts who have joined the Entrepreneurship Development Cell (E-Cell) at our institution. It is my privilege to serve as the Faculty Advisor for this dynamic and innovative initiative.
The E-Cell is a vibrant hub where ideas transform into ventures, and dreams evolve into realities. As a faculty advisor, I am committed to supporting and guiding you through your entrepreneurial journey. Our mission is to foster a culture of entrepreneurship, innovation, and creative problem-solving.
Through a range of workshops, seminars, and events, we aim to equip you with the knowledge and skills needed to navigate the complexities of entrepreneurship. Whether you're a budding entrepreneur with a groundbreaking idea or an experienced professional looking to share your insights, E-Cell is the platform for you.
I encourage you to take full advantage of the resources and opportunities provided by the E-Cell. Engage in networking sessions, collaborate on innovative projects, and participate in business plan competitions. Our team is here to assist you in realizing your entrepreneurial aspirations.
The entrepreneurial spirit thrives when individuals from diverse backgrounds come together to create something extraordinary. We, at the E-Cell, believe in the power of collective intelligence and are excited to witness your growth and achievements.

Wishing you a journey filled with inspiration, innovation, and success.
